Getting Started

Chroma Lab is a tool designed to augment your ability to explore and discover aesthetically pleasing color schemes with help from a genetic algorithm.

To begin the process select the swatch that most appeals to you. Every time you click, a new generation is born. Swatches are ranked top to bottom and left to right based on their similarity to your last choice. The result is that the top left corner is most similar to your choice and the bottom right corner is the least similar, with respect to distance in RGB color space. A user's selections will have a cumulative effect on subsequent generations. Once you steer close to how you want your colors, try selecting the same swatch for several generations to saturate the population with its genes and see more specific variations before finalizing your choice.

How does it work?

  • Chroma Lab uses a genetic algorithm to generate potential color schemes by interpolating between the two parents' colors.
  • The human (that's you!) provides the algorithm's fitness function by selecting a single color scheme as the best of each generation.

Negotiating the problem space to arrive at a solution is a principle task in design that humans perform through an iterative ideation process guided by a design-sensibiity heuristic. My hope is that a tool like this can assist with exploring the problem space for a design solution by allowing humans and computers to contribute their strengths to the process. As a result the designer can reallocate the saved effort towards designing and ultimately produce a more refined solution in the same amount of time.
